# CapitalLab Platform - Complete User Flow Guide

## Overview

CapitalLab is a comprehensive platform that streamlines the process of taking a company public through regulatory approval and stock exchange listing. This guide explains the complete journey from initial application to final listing on the Shora Exchange.

---

## The Four Key Roles

### 1. **Issuer (The Company)**
The company seeking to go public and list their shares on the stock exchange.

### 2. **IB Advisor (Investment Banking Advisor)**
Professional advisors who guide companies through the application process and ensure compliance.

### 3. **CMA Regulator (Capital Markets Authority)**
Government regulatory body that reviews and approves applications to protect investors.

### 4. **Shora Exchange**
The stock exchange where approved companies list their shares for public trading.

---

## Complete User Journey

### Phase 1: Company Registration & Application Start

**Who:** Issuer (Company)

**What Happens:**

1. **Company Signs Up**
   - The company creates an account on CapitalLab
   - They provide basic company information
   - A company profile is created in the system

2. **Team Setup**
   - The CEO or authorized person invites team members
   - Team members can include: CFO, Legal Counsel, Compliance Officer
   - Each team member gets their own login credentials
   - Team members can collaborate on different sections of the application

3. **Application Creation**
   - The company starts a new listing application
   - The system generates a unique application number
   - The application is saved as "Draft" status

---

### Phase 2: Application Preparation

**Who:** Issuer (Company) with optional IB Advisor support

**What Happens:**

1. **Completing Application Sections**
   
   The company fills out 10 major sections:
   
   - **Company Identity**: Basic company information, registration details, contact information
   - **Share Ownership**: Details about current shareholders and ownership structure
   - **Capitalization**: Information about company capital, shares, and financial structure
   - **Governance**: Board of directors, management team, corporate governance policies
   - **Compliance**: Legal compliance, regulatory history, any past violations
   - **Offer Details**: Details about the shares being offered to the public
   - **Prospectus**: Comprehensive document describing the company and the offering
   - **Publication**: Information about how the offering will be advertised
   - **Undertakings**: Commitments the company makes to regulators and investors
   - **Declarations**: Legal declarations and certifications

2. **Document Upload**
   - Each section requires supporting documents
   - Documents can include: certificates, financial statements, legal agreements, board resolutions
   - The system tracks which documents are uploaded and which are missing
   - Documents are securely stored and can be viewed later

3. **Progress Tracking**
   - The system automatically calculates completion percentage
   - Companies can see which sections are complete and which need work
   - Team members can save progress and return later
   - Auto-save feature prevents data loss

4. **Choosing an IB Advisor (Optional but Recommended)**
   - Companies can browse available IB Advisors
   - They can send a request to an IB Advisor for assistance
   - IB Advisors can accept or decline the request
   - Once accepted, the IB Advisor gets access to review and guide the application

---

### Phase 3: IB Advisor Review & Guidance

**Who:** IB Advisor (Investment Banking Professional)

**What Happens:**

1. **Application Access**
   - IB Advisor receives notification of new assignment
   - They log into their IB Advisor dashboard
   - They can see all applications assigned to them

2. **Review Process**
   - IB Advisor reviews each section of the application
   - They check for completeness, accuracy, and compliance
   - They verify all required documents are uploaded
   - They ensure information meets regulatory standards

3. **Providing Feedback**
   - IB Advisor can add comments and suggestions to any section
   - They can request additional documents or clarifications
   - They can mark sections as "Needs Revision" or "Approved"
   - Feedback is sent back to the company team

4. **Collaboration**
   - Company and IB Advisor work together to address issues
   - Multiple rounds of review and revision may occur
   - IB Advisor ensures the application is "CMA-ready"

5. **Final Approval**
   - Once satisfied, IB Advisor marks the application as ready
   - They provide a recommendation for CMA submission
   - Application status changes to "Ready for CMA Submission"

---

### Phase 4: Submission to CMA

**Who:** Issuer (Company) with IB Advisor endorsement

**What Happens:**

1. **Pre-Submission Check**
   - System verifies all sections are 100% complete
   - System confirms all required documents are uploaded
   - System checks that IB Advisor has approved (if applicable)

2. **Formal Submission**
   - Company clicks "Submit to CMA" button
   - Application is locked - no further edits allowed
   - Application status changes to "Submitted to CMA"
   - CMA receives automatic notification

3. **Submission Confirmation**
   - Company receives confirmation with submission date and time
   - Application number is officially registered with CMA
   - Company can track application status in their dashboard

---

### Phase 5: CMA Regulatory Review

**Who:** CMA Regulator (Capital Markets Authority Officer)

**What Happens:**

1. **Application Assignment**
   - CMA receives the application in their queue
   - A CMA Officer is assigned to review the application
   - Officer receives notification and access to all application materials

2. **Initial Review**
   - CMA Officer reviews all 10 sections thoroughly
   - They examine all uploaded documents
   - They verify compliance with capital markets regulations
   - They check for any red flags or concerns

3. **Detailed Analysis**
   - **Financial Review**: Examining company financials, capitalization, and offer structure
   - **Legal Review**: Checking legal compliance, governance, and declarations
   - **Risk Assessment**: Identifying potential risks to investors
   - **Market Impact**: Assessing the offering's impact on the market

4. **Requesting Additional Information**
   - If CMA needs clarification, they send feedback requests
   - Feedback can be sent for specific sections or documents
   - Application status changes to "Under Review - Information Requested"
   - Company receives notification to provide additional information

5. **Company Response**
   - Company logs in to view CMA feedback
   - They can upload additional documents
   - They can provide written responses to questions
   - They resubmit the updated information to CMA

6. **Multiple Review Rounds**
   - CMA may request information multiple times
   - Each round of feedback and response is tracked
   - Communication history is maintained in the system

7. **Final Decision**
   
   CMA makes one of three decisions:
   
   - **Approved**: Application meets all requirements
   - **Conditionally Approved**: Approved with specific conditions to be met
   - **Rejected**: Application does not meet requirements

8. **Approval Process**
   - If approved, CMA issues formal approval letter
   - Approval document is uploaded to the system
   - Application status changes to "CMA Approved"
   - Company and IB Advisor receive approval notification

---

### Phase 6: Shora Exchange Listing

**Who:** Shora Exchange & Issuer (Company)

**What Happens:**

1. **Listing Application**
   - With CMA approval, company applies to Shora Exchange
   - They submit CMA approval documents
   - They provide additional exchange-specific information
   - Listing fee is paid

2. **Exchange Review**
   - Shora Exchange reviews the CMA-approved application
   - They verify the company meets listing requirements
   - They check trading symbol availability
   - They confirm technical readiness for trading

3. **Listing Agreement**
   - Company signs listing agreement with Shora Exchange
   - Agreement outlines ongoing obligations and responsibilities
   - Company commits to disclosure and reporting requirements

4. **Pre-Listing Preparation**
   - Trading symbol is assigned
   - Opening price is determined
   - Market makers are arranged (if needed)
   - Listing date is scheduled

5. **Public Announcement**
   - Listing is announced to the market
   - Prospectus is made available to potential investors
   - Marketing and investor relations activities begin

6. **Listing Day**
   - Company shares begin trading on Shora Exchange
   - Opening ceremony may be held
   - Trading is monitored for smooth operation
   - Company is now publicly listed!

## Application Status Flow

Here's how the application status changes throughout the journey:

1. **Draft** → Company is preparing the application
2. **In Progress** → Company is actively filling out sections
3. **With IB Advisor** → IB Advisor is reviewing
4. **Ready for Submission** → All sections complete, ready for CMA
5. **Submitted to CMA** → Formally submitted to regulator
6. **Under CMA Review** → CMA is reviewing the application
7. **Information Requested** → CMA needs additional information
8. **Under Final Review** → CMA is making final decision
9. **CMA Approved** → Regulatory approval granted
10. **Listed on Shora** → Trading on stock exchange

## Timeline Expectations

**Typical Journey Duration:**

- **Application Preparation**: 2-4 weeks (with IB Advisor support)
- **IB Advisor Review**: 1-2 weeks
- **CMA Review**: 4-8 weeks (varies by complexity)
- **Shora Exchange Listing**: 2-3 weeks after CMA approval

**Total Time**: Approximately 3-4 months from start to listing

*Note: Timeline varies based on application complexity, completeness, and any issues that arise during review.*

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: How long does the entire process take?**
A: Typically 3-4 months from application start to listing, though this varies.

**Q: Can I save my progress and return later?**
A: Yes, the platform auto-saves your work continuously.

**Q: Can multiple people work on the same application?**
A: Yes, team collaboration is fully supported.

### For Companies

**Q: Do I need an IB Advisor?**
A: While not mandatory, IB Advisors significantly improve your chances of approval.

**Q: What happens if my application is rejected?**
A: You can address the issues and resubmit a new application.

**Q: Can I edit my application after submission?**
A: No, but you can respond to CMA feedback requests with additional information.

### For IB Advisors

**Q: How many applications can I manage?**
A: There's no limit, but we recommend managing a workload you can handle effectively.

**Q: Can I decline a client request?**
A: Yes, you can accept or decline any assignment request.

### For CMA Regulators

**Q: How are applications assigned?**
A: Applications are assigned based on officer workload and expertise.

**Q: Can I reassign an application?**
A: Yes, supervisors can reassign applications as needed.
